# X Short-Standard Post Framework

- **Bucket:** short-standard
- **Length:** 101–280 characters, including spaces
- **Primary job:** daily-driver engagement, reply velocity, concise proof, event/action clarity
- **Use for:** updates, quick tips, event reminders, visual captions, direct CTAs, community
  prompts
- **Do not use for:** complex arguments, proof dumps, evergreen essays

## When to choose this bucket

Use short-standard when the reader needs one proof beat plus one action or interpretation: an
event reminder with verified logistics, a visual post where the caption adds context, a proof
receipt with one takeaway, a simple onboarding explanation, or a community question with a real
reason to answer.

## Core shape

Use 2–4 tight lines:

```text
[What changed or what is happening]
[Why it matters / what the proof means]
[What to do, notice, or answer]
```

If visual-heavy:

```text
[Visual noun] is the proof.
[Specific detail in the image/video]
[What it tells you about the world/community]
```

## Archetype fit

Best for announcement, ship-update, milestone, gratitude/shout-out, visual-showcase,
single-answer engagement, and (only when the deadline/limit is real and approved) scarcity posts.

## Anti-patterns (fail the draft)

- Exceeds 280 characters (`rule.platform.limits`).
- More than one CTA.
- Explains lore without a concrete noun.
- Mentions rewards, dates, mechanics, or eligibility without verification
  (`rule.core.claims-safety`).
- Turns a visual post into an essay.
- Fake scarcity or stale-campaign claims.
- Generic, brand-substitutable copy (`rule.core.voice-register`).

## Writer checklist

- 101–280 characters including spaces.
- Hook appears in the first line.
- One receipt or visual detail is present; one reason to care is present.
- At most one CTA / reply path.
- No hashtags (Twitter/X); no mid-sentence em dash.

## Example (brand: Acme Cosmos)

```text
The Acme Cosmos beta wrapped with 60 builders shipping live demos.
Every demo went out as a working link, not a screenshot.
Want in on the next cohort? Reply with what you'd ship.
```
