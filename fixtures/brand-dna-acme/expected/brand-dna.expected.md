## 1. Who we are (one paragraph)

Acme Cosmos makes a small, well-made consumer space telescope and a companion star-charting
app for first-time backyard astronomers. The corpus shows one consistent through-line: help a
beginner find their first real object tonight, then make it easy to come back. The voice is a
knowledgeable friend at the eyepiece — practical, warm, and never mystical.

## 2. Audience

- **Primary:** curious adults buying their first real telescope; put off by jargon.
- **Secondary:** parents setting one up with a kid; lapsed hobbyists returning.
- **Not our audience:** spec-chasing astrophotographers.

## 3. Voice & tone (derived from the own-corpus)

- **Plain and earned.** Short sentences, concrete nouns; the object, the time, the place are named.
- **Confident without hype.** The strongest own posts avoid "best/revolutionary"; the analysis
  flags those words as a competitor tell, not ours.
- **Useful first.** Most posts leave the reader able to do one thing tonight.
- **Calm about failure.** Clouds, drift, dead batteries — said plainly.
- **We, not you-the-customer.**

### Signature vocabulary (top own-corpus terms)
first light, eyepiece, the field, tonight, find, point, focus, clear sky, low horizon, terminator.

### Words we avoid (own-corpus absent; competitor-frequent)
revolutionary, best ever, last chance, only N left, wen, soon, manufactured urgency, astrology framing.
