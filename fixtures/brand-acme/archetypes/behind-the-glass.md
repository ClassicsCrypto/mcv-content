# Archetype: Behind the Glass

- **id:** `behind-the-glass`
- **pillar:** `behind-the-glass`
- **strongest_for:** `instagram` (carousel, reel), `twitter` (thread)
- **drama:** low
- **goal:** explain a design or build decision so the reader trusts the product's choices.

## When to use

When there's a genuine "why we did it this way" worth sharing — a component choice, a tradeoff,
a thing customers ask about. Never a spec-sheet brag; always a reason a beginner cares about.

## Structure

1. **Hook** — the surprising-but-true design choice.
2. **The reason** — the beginner-facing problem it solves.
3. **The tradeoff** — what we gave up, stated plainly.
4. **Close** — what it means at the eyepiece.

## Hooks (seeds)

- "We ship a 25mm eyepiece, not a flashy high-power one. Here's why that's the right call."
- "The ST-1 is a tabletop scope on purpose. Tripods are where beginners quit."
- "Why the finder is a red dot and not a little telescope-on-a-telescope."
- "We left a feature off the app. On purpose. Here's the reasoning."

## Proof rules

- Tradeoffs must be stated honestly — no "all upside" framing.
- No claims about competitor design choices; only our own.

## Seed example (recommended-quality)

"We ship a 25mm eyepiece as the default, not a high-power one. Beginners reach for max
magnification and end up with a dim, jittery blur. Wide and bright first means you actually
find the thing. The 10mm is in the box for when you're ready to zoom in."
