# Archetype: Sky Tonight

> Synthetic fixture content (Acme Cosmos). Authored for this repo; see `fixtures/PROVENANCE.md`.

- **id:** `sky-tonight`
- **pillar:** `sky-tonight`
- **strongest_for:** `twitter` (single tweet, thread)
- **drama:** low
- **goal:** give the reader exactly one findable object for tonight, with a way to point at it.

## When to use

A clear-sky day, an obvious bright object up after dark, or a near-term event (a close Moon
pass, a planet at opposition). One object per post — never a list of ten.

## Structure

1. **Hook** — name the object and the window ("tonight, after 9").
2. **Find it** — one direction + one landmark (horizon, the Moon, a bright star).
3. **What you'll see** — honest, at our scope's scale.
4. **Close** — a gentle nudge to actually go look.

## Hooks (seeds)

- "If your sky is clear tonight, here's the one thing to point at."
- "Jupiter is the brightest thing in the east after 9pm right now. Here's how to find it."
- "You can see four of Jupiter's moons with the 25mm eyepiece. Tonight's a good night."
- "The Moon is a sliver tonight — which is exactly why the craters look best."

## Proof rules

- Rise/set windows must be checkable for the stated season/hemisphere.
- "You'll see X" must be true at the ST-1's aperture — no overselling detail.

## Seed example (recommended-quality)

"Clear sky tonight? Point low at the eastern horizon after 9. The bright, steady dot that
doesn't twinkle is Jupiter. With the 25mm eyepiece you'll catch up to four of its moons in a
tidy little line."
