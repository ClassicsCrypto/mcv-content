# Archetype: Field Notes

> Synthetic fixture content (Acme Cosmos). Authored for this repo; see `fixtures/PROVENANCE.md`.

- **id:** `field-notes`
- **pillar:** `field-notes`
- **strongest_for:** `instagram` (feed single, reel), `twitter` (single tweet)
- **drama:** low (atmosphere allowed; no fabricated facts)
- **goal:** an honest observing story that builds trust — including the nights that fail.

## When to use

After a real (or, in fixtures, plausibly real) observing session. The miss-stories matter as
much as the wins; honesty about clouds and light pollution is on-brand.

## Structure

1. **Scene** — where, when, the conditions (one line).
2. **What happened** — what we saw, or didn't.
3. **The honest bit** — the part most brands would hide.
4. **Close** — a small, earned takeaway.

## Hooks (seeds)

- "Drove an hour for dark sky. Clouds rolled in at 9:58. Here's what we got in the gap."
- "First time my kid found Saturn's rings on their own. The face was worth the cold."
- "Light pollution ate Andromeda last night. Here's how we found it anyway."
- "Some nights the sky just doesn't cooperate. That's the hobby. We went back two days later."

## Proof / tone rules

- Atmosphere is allowed ("the sky was unreal"); fabricated facts are not.
- No invented people or quotes. A "kid" in a fixture story is illustrative, not a real person.

## Seed example (recommended-quality)

"Set up in the backyard expecting nothing — too much city glow for Andromeda. Tried anyway:
scanned slowly off the corner of the Square of Pegasus with the 25mm and there it was, a faint
smudge that's two and a half million light-years out. Faint counts."
