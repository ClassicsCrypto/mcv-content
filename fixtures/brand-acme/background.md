# Background — Acme Cosmos

> Synthetic fixture brand. Authored for this repository; see `fixtures/PROVENANCE.md`. No real
> company, product, person, or account is referenced or implied.

## Product

- **Acme Cosmos ST-1** — a 130mm tabletop reflector telescope aimed at first-time observers.
  Ships with two eyepieces (25mm + 10mm), a red-dot finder, and a quick-collimation card.
- **Acme Cosmos app** — a free companion star chart with a "tonight" feed, a guided
  first-light walkthrough, and a saved-objects log.

## Story

Acme Cosmos started as a fictional weekend project: a small team that kept lending their own
scope to friends and noticed the same thing every time — people gave up in the first ten
minutes because nothing in the box told them *what to point at tonight*. The ST-1 and the app
are the answer to that one problem: a sharp, forgiving first scope plus a feed that always has
an answer to "okay, now what do I look at?"

## What matters to the brand

1. **First light, fast.** A new owner should see a real object the first clear night.
2. **Honesty about conditions.** Clouds, light pollution, and the Moon all matter; we say so.
3. **No mysticism.** Astronomy, not astrology. The sky is enough.

## Operating cadence (fixture)

- Twitter is the primary lane (text-heavy `how-to` and `sky-tonight`).
- Instagram carries the visual `field-notes` and `behind-the-glass` lanes.
- Giphy carries short reaction loops for community replies.

## Sample objects we talk about (all real, public, free-to-mention sky objects)

The Moon, Jupiter and its moons, Saturn's rings, the Orion Nebula (M42), the Pleiades (M45),
the Andromeda Galaxy (M31), the Double Cluster, and the summer Milky Way. These are public
astronomical objects, not brand or third-party IP.
