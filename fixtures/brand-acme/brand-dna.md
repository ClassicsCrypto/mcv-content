`brand_id`: `acme-cosmos`

## 1. Who we are (one paragraph)

Acme Cosmos makes a small, well-made consumer space telescope and a companion star-charting
app for first-time backyard astronomers. We help people find their first real object in the
night sky and keep coming back. We sound like a knowledgeable friend who has set the scope up
in a cold field a hundred times — practical, warm, never mystical about it.

## 2. Audience

- **Primary:** curious adults (25–55) buying their first "real" telescope; intimidated by jargon.
- **Secondary:** parents setting one up with a kid; lapsed hobbyists returning after years.
- **Not our audience:** competitive astrophotographers chasing sub-arcsecond gear specs.

We write *to* the beginner in the field at 10pm, not *about* them to an industry.

## 3. Voice & tone

- **Plain and earned.** Short sentences. Concrete nouns. We name the object, the time, the place.
- **Confident without hype.** We never say "best," "revolutionary," or "game-changing."
- **Useful first.** Every post should leave the reader able to *do* one thing tonight.
- **Calm about failure.** Clouds happen, alignment drifts, batteries die. We say so.
- **We, not you-the-customer.** We stand next to the reader at the eyepiece.

### Words we use
first light, eyepiece, alignment, the field, tonight, find, point, focus, clear sky, low horizon.

### Words we avoid
unlock, supercharge, revolutionary, game-changer, "the best," exclamation stacks, FOMO,
manufactured urgency, mystical/astrology framing, "limited drop" language.

## 4. Drama dial

**low.** Acme Cosmos is a `brand` account. Claims stay literal and falsifiable. Atmosphere is
fine ("the sky was unreal last night"); fabricated causal claims, invented superlatives, and
manufactured urgency are not. This matches `drama_dial: "low"` in `brand.json` and the voice
rules in `rules/core`.

## 5. Proof & specificity rules

- Name the object (e.g. "the Orion Nebula"), the date/season, and the conditions when relevant.
- If we cite a number, it must be checkable (focal length, magnitude, a published rise time).
- No invented testimonials, sales figures, or "thousands of customers" claims.
- A how-to claim must actually work with our product as shipped.

## 6. Pillars (content lanes)

| Pillar | What it is | Example angle |
|---|---|---|
| `sky-tonight` | What's visible tonight and how to find it | "Find Jupiter low in the east after 9pm" |
| `how-to` | Setup, alignment, focus, care | "Three minutes to a sharp Moon" |
| `field-notes` | Honest observing stories, including misses | "The night the clouds won" |
| `behind-the-glass` | How the scope/app are made and why | "Why we ship a 25mm eyepiece, not a 6mm" |

## 7. Strongest lane

`how-to` is the strongest lane: it is the most shareable and the most defensible, because every
claim is something the reader can verify at the eyepiece the same night.

## 8. Hard nos

- No astrology / horoscope framing.
- No "buy now / last chance" urgency unless a real, dated promotion exists.
- No claims about competitor products.
- No images with embedded marketing text baked into the frame (visual gate will flag it).
